📄  Published · BMC Bioinformatics 2026
Benchmarking of Methods to Analyse GBS-MeDIP Data

GBS-MeDIP is a cost-effective method combining genotyping-by-sequencing and methylated DNA immunoprecipitation — but its unique output makes conventional pipelines inadequate. This project benchmarks multiple statistical approaches — EdgeR, DESeq2, limma and Mann-Whitney — across chicken, dog and pig datasets. FeatureCounts was identified as the most reliable tool for count matrix generation, and Mann-Whitney delivered the lowest false-positive rate for differential methylation analysis.

R de Anca Prado et al., 2026
🧬  PhD Main Project
Evolution of SNPs in Relation to Methylation in an Obesogenic Environment

The mechanisms by which early-life metabolic environments alter genome stability remain largely unknown. This project investigates the co-emergence of SNPs and DNA methylation changes in a childhood obesity mouse model, using GBS-MeDIP to examine whether epigenetic marks at specific loci precede or accompany the appearance of genetic variants across generations — contributing to our understanding of how obesogenic environments may reshape the genome.
